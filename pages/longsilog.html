Longsilog

Ingredients

  • Longanisa
    • 1 pack of longanisa
    • ½ cup of water
  • Sinangag (Garlic Fried Rice)
    • 2 cups of day-old cooked jasmine rice (or any rice of choice)
    • 4 cloves of garlic
    • 3 tsp of cooking oil of choice
    • 2 tsp of salt
    • 2 tsp of black pepper
  • 4 Eggs (Itlog)

Ingredients

  1. Pour ½ cup of water into a pan and bring to a light boil on low to medium heat
  2. Add longanisa to the boiling water and cook for about 20 minutes or until all of the water has evaporated
  3. While allowing longanisa to boil, mince 4 cloves of garlic for the sinangag
  4. Continue frying on pan until the sausages are darkened/caramelized all around
  5. Separate links by cutting with scissors or a fork
  6. Turn off stove and cover pan with aluminum foil while cooking the rest of the dish
  7. In a separate pan, add 3 tsp of cooking oil and turn to medium heat
  8. When the oil is heated, add minced garlic to the pan and cook until golden brown
    1. Make sure that the oil is not too hot to ensure that the garlic does not burn
  9. Add the day-old rice to the pan and stirfry for 3-5 minutes, making sure that rice is coated with oil and garlic is mixed with rice well
    1. The day-old rice prevents the rice from breaking apart during a second cooking
  10. Turn off heat and add salt and pepper to the rice, giving the sinagnag a final mix
  11. Transfer sinangag to plates and use the same pan to fry two eggs
  12. Add salt, pepper, and parsley to the eggs
  13. Transfer the longanisa and fried eggs to the plates
    1. Can be served with Filipino banana sauce (banana ketchup)
